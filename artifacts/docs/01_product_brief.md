# PUNCHLINE — Product Brief

## One-line pitch

A web-first multiplayer party game platform with live rooms, async daily rounds, and a living card/content system.

## Clean positioning

Do **not** position this as “AI Cards Against Humanity.” That makes the product sound like a shallow clone.

Position it as:

> A real-time multiplayer game engine that later becomes a living content platform.

## Product thesis

Party games are fun, but most web versions fail because they are either static, janky, real-time-only, or unmoderated. PUNCHLINE starts by making the multiplayer room feel smooth and reliable, then adds retention through daily asynchronous rounds, and finally adds freshness through a content platform.

## North star

> The party game that works whether your friends are online now, online later, or not online at all.

## Primary wedge

Remote friend groups who already hang out on Discord, WhatsApp, Zoom, FaceTime, or group chats.

The first product should solve one problem extremely well:

> “We want to start a funny game instantly, with no install, and not have it break when someone disconnects.”

## Product pillars

1. **Smooth live rooms** — instant join, phone-first, reconnectable, server-authoritative.
2. **Play on your schedule** — async daily mode keeps the group alive between live sessions.
3. **Fresh content** — content becomes a service, not a fixed deck.
4. **Safe by default, flexible by room** — content tiers, house rules, reporting, moderation.

## What makes this worth building

This is a stronger portfolio product than a CRUD app because it forces you to build:

- real-time room orchestration
- WebSocket state synchronization
- server-authoritative game transitions
- guest identity and reconnectable sessions
- durable game results
- async workflows
- content moderation flows
- AI-assisted content generation pipeline

## Anti-goals

Do not start with:

- AI everything
- marketplace
- stream mode
- solo mode
- complicated accounts
- global social network
- monetization
- complex moderation dashboard

The first version should feel narrow and good.
